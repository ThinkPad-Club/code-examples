# Classroom Conduct 🫡

We want ThinkPad Club to be **fun and focused** so everyone has an equal chance to learn, build projects and write great code. These rules help create a classroom where everyone can concentrate, solve problems, and enjoy learning new technology. When we all follow the rules, the class runs smoothly and everyone has a fair chance to **focus, code, and learn**.

## Our Class Motto

Sometimes the class may get noisy or distracted. When we need to refocus our attention, we will say our class motto together to reset the room and get ready to learn again.

- When I say: **"Why are we here, ThinkPad Club?"**
- Everyone says: **"To focus, code and learn!"**

## Our Class Conduct Rules

During ThinkPad Club we want everyone to stay focused and respectful so the coding demonstrations and projects go smoothly. These rules help students concentrate on their work and make sure no one distracts others who are trying to learn.

1. **Listen and work during coding demonstrations**
2. **Wear headphones to focus on work after demonstrations**
4. **Stay in your seat and focus on your own project**  
5. **Raise your hand if you need a break, snack, or bathroom**  
6. **Work only on your own computer**  
7. **Keep hands to yourself at all times**  
8. **Use inside voices**
9. **Do not distract other students**
10. **No unapproved games, websites or videos**

## Three Strikes

If a student repeatedly breaks our class conduct rules and / or distracts other students they may receive a **strike**, which may be tracked on the board. Strikes help remind students to reset their behavior and return to focusing on their own work. Students who receive one or more strikes may lose any **Gold Star status** they may have for that day. If a student receives **three strikes**, they may need to take a longer break and return to **aftercare for 15 minutes or longer**. This helps keep the classroom calm so all students have an equal opportunity to **focus, code, and learn**.

## If You Need a Break

Sometimes everyone needs a short break. If you feel restless, tired, bored, have too much energy or need to use the bathroom you can **raise your hand** and ask to take a break. Only **one student may take a break at a time**, and breaks should usually last **5–10 minutes** so everyone gets a fair chance. During a break you may do one or all of the following.

- Go to the bathroom  
- Take a short walk around the lunch area  
- Get water from the water fountain  
- Eat a snack from your lunch cooler
