# Optional Materials for Home Learning 💻

Students can participate fully in ThinkPad Club using their **school Chromebooks**.

However, some **special projects and elective stations** in the club require a full laptop computer. These may include:

- LEGO robotics projects
- Game Boy game development
- Raspberry Pi server projects
- Linux programming activities
- certain Python and development tools

Because of this, families who want to support coding practice at home may consider purchasing a **used ThinkPad laptop**.

Used or refurbished computers are **perfectly fine** and often cost **$100–$200**.

The most important requirement is:

⚠️ **At least 8GB of RAM**

---

# Recommended Option 1  
## Small Kid-Friendly Laptop: ThinkPad 11e (Touchscreen)

This is a great option for younger students.

Why we like it:

- Small and easy for kids to carry
- Durable school-style laptop
- Touchscreen is helpful for some creative tools
- Usually inexpensive when purchased used

Used ThinkPad 11e laptops can often be found for **$100–$200**.

Example places to look:

- ThinkPad 11e on Amazon
- ThinkPad 11e on eBay
- ThinkPad 11e on Newegg

Just make sure the computer has **8GB of RAM or more**.

---

# Recommended Option 2  
## More Powerful Laptop: ThinkPad T470

The **ThinkPad T470** is a stronger laptop that can handle larger projects.

This may be a better long-term option for:

- older students
- students who want to do more advanced programming
- students interested in robotics, servers, or game development

Pros:

- More powerful
- Very durable
- Professional-grade laptop

Cons:

- Heavier
- No touchscreen
- Usually a little more expensive

Example places to look:

- ThinkPad T470 on Amazon
- ThinkPad T470 on Newegg

---

# Important Note

These computers are **completely optional**.

Students can still participate in ThinkPad Club using their **school Chromebook**.

The laptops above simply make it easier to explore **certain advanced projects at home**.
